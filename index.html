Betty Boop - A History

Betty Boop began as both a parody and a powerful symbol of unabashed sexuality, a combination she would retain, to varying degrees, throughout her lifespan in the media. She first appeared in 1930 as an anthropomorphic cartoon french poodle in the short Dizzy Dishes, where she sang, danced, and wagged her ears. A year later, she had transitioned into a human character, her flappy ears morphing into her now-famous hoop earrings. At once ingenuous, gentle, and kind, she was a female figure who stood out in the world of American animation and comics; whereas early characters like Minnie Mouse were often largely just copies of male figures in women’s clothing, Betty Boop was unique. Unlike Olive Oyl in “Popeye” or Minnie Mouse, she wasn’t defined by her relation to a more famous male character; she was the main figure all on her own. Origanlly based on an American singer Helen Kane; however it came to light that Helen had based her act on African American signer Baby Esther Jones, known for her "put-on" baby style voice. Betty Facts

  1. Orignally a canine character; a French Poodle
  2. As a female character she was confident and sexy but was toned down in the 1930's due to the Hays Code
  3. One of the first cartoons to highlight sexual harassment
  4. Her songs promoted jazz and swing, which had origins in Black communities
